Presenter accessibility tips

These reminders help presentations, Q&A, and materials stay accessible for the full room.
Interpretation support: Speak at a steady, moderate pace to support clear and accurate ASL interpretation.
Q&A: Anyone asking a question should state their name before speaking, and presenters should restate each question before responding.
Turn-taking: Keep to one speaker at a time to maintain accessibility.
Printed materials: Bring at least five printed copies of your paper, even if it is a draft, for attendees who want to follow along with the written version.
Large print handouts: Consider large print handouts using a sans-serif font at 16-point size or larger, and let participants know whether you would like materials returned.
Lip-reading access: Face the audience at an angle that makes lip-reading possible and avoid speaking while turned away, looking down at notes, or facing slides.
Describe visuals: Verbally describe the content of slides and any other visual materials you use.
Video accessibility: Any video content should be captioned or subtitled, or accompanied by a transcript.
Digital copies: Whenever possible, share an electronic version of your materials so participants can resize text or use screen readers and text-to-speech tools.
